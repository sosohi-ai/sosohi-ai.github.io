SLICE: Safe-referenced Latent-space Intervention for Concept Erasing in Diffusion Models

Abstract

Abstract

The inadvertent generation of copyrighted and harmful content by diffusion models has emerged as a significant challenge in responsible AI. To address this, we propose SLICE, a post-hoc intervention framework that achieves precise concept erasing by integrating a lightweight embedding layer while keeping the original UNet parameters frozen. SLICE establishes a clear decision criterion through timestep-aware projection and safe-reference anchoring, allowing for the selective suppression of target concepts based on their relative position in the latent space. By leveraging safe concepts as geometric anchors, our strategy suppresses harmful representations while effectively preserving the original prompt's semantic integrity and visual layout. Extensive experiments and human studies across diverse domains demonstrate that SLICE provides a robust and efficient solution for safety-enhanced image generation. Our approach confirms that precise, criterion-based intervention can successfully enhance model safety without compromising creative capabilities.
